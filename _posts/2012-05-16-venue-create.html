Venue data

The first step is similar to show creation.

Location

The location of the venue will be used to direct attendees to the performance location, as well as keep shows indexed by location. You can introduce any address that is recognized by Google Maps. If a certain address is not found, try to make it more concrete by adding elements such as the city or the country.

Did you know? Most of the time it is enough to introduce just the name of the venue and select it from the list of options.

You can also modify the time zone and currency of the venue, that will default to your current time zone and EUR. All performances created for this venue will be considered relative to this time zone and currency.

Auditoriums

Auditoriums are rooms to hold performances in your venue. Your newly created venue comes with one default auditorium with capacity for 100 attendees. You may add or remove new auditoriums, modify the auditorium name or its zones and prices.

An auditorium is a template for future performances; whenever you create a new show and link it to this auditorium the capacity and ticket prices will be automatically filled in for you (still, you may edit these values later).

Combinations

These are solutions for well-known cases:
Case Example Solution
Multiple locations for the same show. Music or Theater Tour. Multiple venues with different locations and time zones.
Multiple ticket prices for the same show. VIP tickets, student discounts, multi-day discounts. Single auditorium with multiple zones applying different prices.
A single venue with multiple possible arrangements. An auditorium that can have seats removed to increase capacity. Multiple auditoriums with different configurations.
The venue can have multiple performances happening at the same time. Theaters with multiple auditoriums. Multiple auditoriums.
